What Biden and Trump Said (and Didn’t Say) in the CNN Presidential Debate

By Katrina Ventura

In a highly anticipated face-off, President Joe Biden and former President Donald Trump participated in their first debate of the 2024 election season on CNN in Atlanta.

The debate, which was marked by a series of sharp exchanges and policy discussions, provided a glimpse into the priorities and rhetorical strategies of both candidates. A detailed analysis of the debate transcript reveals interesting insights into their focus areas and speaking styles.

Trump vs Biden Word Cloud

Joe Biden spoke a total of 3580 words (excluding stop words) during the debate. His speech focused on several key issues ranked below./p>

Biden's transcript reveals a strong emphasis on words related to the economy. He used jobs 13 times, economy 10 times, and tax 9 times. Biden also brought up the topic of childcare 7 times, and climate 6 times.

The CNN transcript also took note of 3 instances in which Biden's speech was inaudible, which is just one more than the number of times Trump was inaudible.

Notably, Biden refrained from directly referring to Trump as "Trump", instead opting to call him "this guy" 14 times, and once referred to his opponent as a child.

TRUMP: Let’s not act like children.
BIDEN: You are a child.

In contrast to Biden, Trump's top key mentions revolve around international affairs. His speech also included 15 mentions of Russia, 12 mentions of Ukraine and 11 mentions of China.

Trump concentrated heavily on the border, mentioning it 24 times, which underscores his focus on immigration policies—a cornerstone of his previous administration.

At one point in the debate, Trump brought up Russia in the context of disinformation as he defended himself for having been quoted in a previous article by The Atlantic for referring to Americans who died in the was as 'losers and suckers'.

TRUMP: It was made up by him, just like Russia, Russia, Russia was made up, just like the 51 intelligence agents are made up, just like the new thing with the 16 economists are talking.

Words Unsaid

While the words they chose to emphasize provided insight into their policy focuses, the words left unsaid were equally telling.

On questions surrounding the ongoing conflict in Gaza, Biden mentions Israel 6 times but not once uttered the word Palestinian or Palestine referring only to Hamas. When asked about his government's response to the conflict, Biden talked about the ceasefire plan and talked about Israel and Netanyahu and referred to the Palestinians as innocent people.

Trump, on the other hand, not once said the word healthcare during the debate, and instead switched between Medicare and Medicaid. He however mentions abortion, but not once used the word childcare.

The first debate of the 2024 election season provided a clear contrast between Biden and Trump's priorities and rhetorical strategies. However, the real test lies in their actions.

As the election season progresses, it will be crucial to scrutinize how these verbal commitments translate into tangible policies and their respective party's platforms.
